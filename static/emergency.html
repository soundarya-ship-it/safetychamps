🩺 First aid — golden hour

General accident steps (always follow)
Step 1: Stop bleeding

Press firmly on the wound with any clean cloth. Hold pressure — do not lift to check. If blood soaks through, add more cloth on top, do not replace.

Step 2: Keep the person still

Do NOT move someone with a head/neck/spine injury unless they are in immediate danger (fire, traffic). Movement can paralyse.

Step 3: Check breathing

Look at chest. Listen at mouth. Feel for breath on your cheek. If not breathing, begin chest compressions: centre of chest, 5 cm deep, 100 per minute, do not stop until ambulance arrives.

Step 4: Keep warm + reassure

Cover with a coat or blanket. Talk to them — keep them awake if possible. Loss of consciousness needs urgent hospitalisation.

❌ Do NOT give food or water — risk of choking if surgery needed.
❌ Do NOT move someone bleeding from ears/nose — possible skull fracture.
❌ Do NOT remove embedded objects — pack around them.
CPR (no pulse, not breathing)
Hand position

Heel of one hand at the centre of chest. Other hand on top, fingers interlocked. Arms straight.

Depth and rate

Push down 5–6 cm. Let chest fully recoil. 100–120 compressions per minute. Hum "Stayin' Alive" — that's the rhythm.

Continue without pausing

If alone, just compressions — no breaths. Do not stop until ambulance arrives or person breathes.

Severe bleeding
Direct pressure

Firm, continuous pressure with palm + clean cloth. 10 minutes minimum without lifting.

Elevate if possible

Raise the wounded limb above heart level if no fracture suspected.

Tourniquet — last resort

Only for life-threatening limb bleeding that won't stop. 5 cm above wound, never on joints. Note the time.

Spinal / neck injury
Do NOT move

Support the head and neck in the position found. Hold gently with both hands.

If you must move (fire/traffic)

Log roll: 3+ people, keep head, neck, and spine in one straight line.
